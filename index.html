Maryam Mirzakhani

First Woman To Win The Fields Medal

Timeline

  • 1977 - Born in Tehran, Iran
  • 1994 - Became the first Iranian female who win a gold medal at the International Mathematical Olympiad
  • 1998 - After attending a conference consisting former Olympiad competitors, the bus fell off a cliff, killing seven of the university students. Mirzakhani was one of the few survivors
  • 1999 - Obtained BSc in mathematics from the Sharif University of Technology
  • 2004 - Earned Phd from Harvard University
  • 2009 - Became a professor in Stanford University
  • 2014 - Awarded the Fields Medal for "her outstanding contributions to the dynamics and geometry of Riemann surfaces and their moduli spaces" and became first woman who received the Fields medal
  • 2017 - Died from cancer
  • 2017 - The high school that Mirzakhani formerly attended â€“ named their amphitheater and library after her. Additionally, The University wherein Mirzakhani obtained her bachelors, has since named their main library in the College of Mathematics after her
  • 2014 - students at the University of Oxford founded the Mirzakhani Society, a society for women and non-binary students studying Mathematics at the University of Oxford
  • 2019 - The Breakthrough Prize Foundation announced that the Maryam Mirzakhani New Frontiers Prize has been created to be awarded to outstanding women in the field of mathematics each year
  • 2020 - on International Day of Women and Girls in STEM, Mirzakhani was honoured by UN Women as one of seven female scientists dead or alive who have shaped the world
